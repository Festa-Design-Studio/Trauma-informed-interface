Usage Guidelines

Usage:

  • At the top of content cards to clearly identify content type
  • Use specific color coding to distinguish between different content categories
  • Place in a consistent location (typically top of card under the image)
  • Keep category names concise and descriptive
  • Use rounded-md for category badges vs. rounded-full for tag pills

Color Coding Guidelines:

  • Azurite blue: Research, educational, and informational content
  • Chrysocolla green: Practical applications, tools, and resources
  • Rhodochrosite rose: Case studies, personal stories, and sensitive content
  • Sunstone orange: Community content and special topics

Accessibility:

  • Ensure sufficient color contrast between text and background
  • Don't rely solely on color to convey meaning
  • Use consistent visual styles across all categories
  • Consider adding icons for enhanced recognition

Trauma-informed Considerations:

  • Clear categorization helps users make informed choices about content
  • Consistent placement builds predictability and reduces cognitive load
  • Soft color palettes reduce visual stress while still providing distinction
  • Subtle borders and rounded corners create a sense of containment and safety
  • Category identification helps users avoid potentially triggering content
