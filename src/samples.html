How Trauma Affects the Brain

Click to play video when ready

You can pause, adjust speed, or enable captions at any time. Transcript available below.

When a person experiences trauma, several areas of the brain can be affected, including the amygdala, hippocampus, and prefrontal cortex. These changes can influence how a person processes information and responds to their environment.

Understanding these neurobiological effects helps us design interfaces that support rather than unintentionally trigger stress responses.

Types of Empathy in Design

Empathy in design can be divided into three main types: cognitive empathy, emotional empathy, and compassionate empathy. Each plays a distinct role in how we understand and respond to user needs.

Cognitive Empathy

Cognitive empathy involves understanding another person's perspective intellectually. It's the ability to recognize what someone might be thinking or feeling without necessarily sharing those emotions.

Emotional Empathy

Emotional empathy is the ability to share or mirror the feelings of another person. This type of empathy involves a deeper connection where you actually feel some of what the other person is experiencing.

Compassionate Empathy

Compassionate empathy goes beyond understanding and feeling—it leads to action. This type of empathy motivates us to help address the needs or challenges that others face.
