Types of Trauma

Trauma is categorized in different ways based on the duration, frequency, and nature of the traumatic events. Understanding these distinctions helps inform how we create more supportive digital experiences.

Acute trauma results from a single incident or event. Examples include accidents, natural disasters, or sudden loss. The impact can be immediate and overwhelming.

Chronic trauma results from prolonged exposure to distressing situations, such as ongoing abuse, neglect, or living in areas with persistent violence.

Complex trauma results from exposure to multiple traumatic events, often of an invasive, interpersonal nature. These experiences typically occur within specific relationships or contexts.

Collective trauma affects entire groups, communities, or societies. Examples include war, genocide, pandemics, or systemic oppression. Its effects can be felt across generations.

Course Learning Template Documentation

Usage

This template is designed for the main learning interface in educational contexts. It provides a structured layout for delivering course content while incorporating trauma-informed design principles.

Key Components

  • Emergency exit button (always accessible)
  • Navigation sidebar with clear progress indicators
  • Content warnings with user controls
  • Accessibility controls for content presentation
  • Support resources prominently displayed
  • Reflection exercises for processing content

Trauma-Informed Considerations

  • Clear content warnings with option to skip potentially triggering content
  • User control over content detail level and presentation
  • Support resources prominently available
  • Calm color palette and generous spacing
  • Consistent navigation with clear indicators of location and progress
  • Option to pause, reflect, or take breaks via the reflection exercise
  • Non-pressuring language throughout the interface
  • Emotional check-in feature to help users process their feelings and take breaks if needed
