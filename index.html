Core Trauma-Informed Principles

Our design system is built on five core principles that ensure every interaction respects the user's emotional wellbeing.

Safety First

All components are designed with psychological safety in mind, including clear exit points, content warnings, and gentle visual design that avoids triggering elements.

Trustworthiness & Transparency

Our components are transparent about their purpose and actions, building trust through predictable behaviors and clear communication.

Choice & User Control

Empowerment is central to our design philosophy, with adjustable settings, opt-in interactions, and multiple navigation paths to give users agency.

Collaboration & Mutuality

Our components encourage co-creation of experiences, with supportive feedback mechanisms and acknowledging the shared responsibility of digital wellbeing.

Empowerment & Skill Building

Components are designed to foster resilience, with supportive messaging, non-judgmental feedback, and design that builds confidence through successful interactions.

Cultural Responsiveness

Our system acknowledges diverse experiences of trauma and incorporates inclusive design practices that respect cultural differences in emotional expression and safety needs.

Atomic Component Hierarchy

Our trauma-informed components are organized following atomic design principles, ensuring consistent implementation of safety across all levels of complexity.

Atoms

The foundational elements of our design system, each designed with trauma-informed considerations.

  • Buttons (Primary, Secondary, Tertiary)
  • Typography (Headings, Body, Captions)
  • Form Elements (Inputs, Checkboxes, Radios)
  • Status Indicators (Badges, Tags, Progress)
  • Media Elements (Icons, Images)
  • Emergency Exit Button
  • Image Container

Molecules

Groups of atoms working together as a functional unit with cohesive trauma-informed properties.

  • Form Groups (Label + Input + Helper Text)
  • Alerts (Content Warnings, Supportive Messages)
  • Card Elements (Headers, Bodies, Footers)
  • Text Groups (Title + Subtitle, List Items)
  • Navigation Elements (Menus, Breadcrumbs)
  • Supportive Message Alerts
  • Card Body
  • Card Footer
  • Video Controls

Organisms

Relatively complex components composed of molecules and atoms that form distinct sections of an interface.

  • Course Components (Module Cards, Progress Trackers)
  • Modal Dialogs (Trauma-Informed Overlays)
  • Blog Components (Article Cards, Content Warnings)
  • Safety Toolkits (Emergency Exits, Resource Centers)

Templates

Page-level structures that organize organisms into comprehensive layouts with consistent trauma-informed navigation patterns.

  • Learning Module Templates
  • Blog Article Templates (Coming Soon)
  • Resource Center Templates (Coming Soon)
